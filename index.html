Food Access Webmap

About this project:

The pressing issue of food access within New Mexico presents a unique set of challenges that merit focused attention and innovative solutions. Grounded in the broader context of urban and rural food deserts, existing literature underscores the critical role of geographical analysis tools in illuminating the dynamics of food availability. Many studies regarding this topic predominantly pivot around datasets from entities like the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA) and incorporate a variety of retail food outlets, including both large grocery retailers, and more commonly smaller local options. Methods that are often used to indicate food access rely on metrics such as: proximity to grocery retailers, income, and transportation. This methodological groundwork set the stage for a nuanced examination of food access, revealing the complexity of factors that contribute to food deserts. Across New Mexico, the intricate web between geographical isolation, socio-economic factors, and limited infrastructure compounds the challenges of securing adequate food access. Rather than relying on standard methodologies used to assess food access our map offers a different approach to the topic by examining the overall abundance and saturation of food sources (often retailers) and categorizes them by type to provide an in depth understanding of what types of retailers service particular areas which often affects the availability of healthy and nutritious foods in different areas. This map is based on SNAP retailers in New Mexico which we filtered for reliability and relevance to our project. This approach seeks to provide a comparative analysis to predominant methodologies used to indicate food access highlighting a dimension of food access that is often not depicted and has potentially valueable for policymakers, community leaders, and stakeholders to devise targeted interventions for improving food security.
